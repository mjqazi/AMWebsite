BIM transition without stalling production.

A municipal infrastructure department needed Revit production support and ISO 19650-aligned standards while its internal team learned the new workflow.

Project Snapshot

Client: municipal infrastructure department, anonymized. Scope: BIM overflow modeling, model setup, BEP support, clash workflow, standards documentation, and practical enablement for 12 internal staff.

The risk

The department had committed to a BIM transition, but live project work did not pause while standards were written and staff trained. Their internal team needed room to learn without missing deliverables. The common failure mode in this situation is trying to turn every production deadline into a training event.

Our role

  • Built template models and naming conventions aligned to the client's emerging BIM Execution Plan.
  • Handled overflow modeling so internal staff could focus on standard adoption and review.
  • Supported clash rounds and issue tracking with repeatable model-coordination steps.
  • Documented practical rules for LOD, federation, file naming, and handover.
  • Ran focused working sessions with staff using live project examples.

Result

100%On-time delivery
6 moBIM rollout
12Staff trained

Production work stayed on schedule while the internal team built competence. By the end of the six-month engagement, the department had a usable BEP, project templates, model review routines, and staff who had practiced the workflow on real deliverables.

The lesson

BIM transition succeeds faster when production support and standards work are treated as one operating system. If standards are written away from live delivery, they often fail on the first real deadline. If production runs without standards, the team simply creates another unmanaged drafting queue.
