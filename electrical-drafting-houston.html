§04 — Houston-Specific FAQ

Questions Gulf Coast engineering managers ask us

Do you work to NEC 2023 and Texas state amendments?
Yes. Every US engagement is delivered to NEC 2023, plus applicable state and local amendments. Texas adopts NEC on a statewide basis, but Houston-area municipalities and counties may have additional amendments; we verify per-project.
Do you handle Class I Division 1/2 hazardous-area classification?
Yes. NEC Article 500 and 505 classification drawings, equipment schedules, and cable & raceway schedules for Class I and Class II hazardous locations. We reference API RP 500 / 505 for petroleum-specific classification and coordinate with your HAZOP outputs.
Can you support Gulf Coast EPC subcontractor workflows?
Yes. Most Houston EPC engagements run as subcontractor arrangements — we act as production drafting inside your title block, invisible to your end client. Standard flow-down NDAs, IP clauses, and deliverable ownership apply.
Can you produce heat-tracing documentation per IEEE 515?
Yes. Heat-tracing layouts, circuit schedules, temperature-maintenance calculations, and control documentation for pipeline freeze protection and process heating per IEEE 515. We support both self-regulating and constant-wattage systems.
What is your turnaround for CST-based clients?
Our Pakistan production team's working hours overlap with Houston morning. Houston 09:00 CST equals Pakistan 20:00 PKT. This gives effectively overnight production — work dispatched end-of-day Houston time, returned before your next-morning stand-up. Standard SLDs deliver in 3–5 business days.
