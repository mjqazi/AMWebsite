§02 — What We Produce

Documentation types for mining projects

HV & MV Substation Documentation

132/11 kV, 66/11 kV, and 33/11 kV substation SLDs, protection schemes, busbar layouts, and AS 2067 / IEEE compliant documentation.

Mine Power Distribution

§04 — Mining FAQ

Questions mining engineering managers ask us

What mining codes do you work to?
AS/NZS 3000 and the AS/NZS 60079 series for Australian mining; NEC 2023 and CSA C22.1 for North American operations; IEC 60364 for international sites. We also maintain operator-specific drafting standards for Rio Tinto, Fortescue, BHP, Anglo American, and Barrick.
Can you handle intrinsically safe (Ex) area classification?
Yes. AS/NZS 60079 and IEC 60079 compliant hazardous-area classification drawings, equipment schedules, and installation details for Zones 0, 1, 2 and dust-zone equivalents 20, 21, 22. We coordinate with your HAZOP outputs.
Do you work with mining EPCs and mid-tier operators as well as tier-1s?
Yes. Our client base spans tier-1 operators, mid-tier mining companies, and EPC contractors serving both. Same quality process; the engagement model scales to project size.
What is your turnaround on mine power distribution documentation?
Single-line diagrams deliver in 3–5 business days. Full MCC and switchgear packages in 2–3 weeks. Shutdown packages in 5–10 business days from scope confirmation. Revision SLA 24 hours on managed engagements.
Do you produce shutdown / outage documentation under time pressure?
Yes. Scope-of-work drawings, isolation schedules, switching documentation, and commissioning packs are a core specialty. We work to compressed shutdown timelines via dedicated teams during planned outage windows.
