Bad drafting doesn't just delay drawings. It costs you this.

Three concrete line items every Engineering Manager sees on the back of a bad vendor choice.

Missing arc-flash label

A single missing or incorrect arc-flash label can trigger an OSHA citation under electrical safety regulations. Citations are public record and show up in insurance underwriting and client vendor reviews.

Every revision cycle on a poorly-drafted SLD consumes senior engineer hours at onshore rates — $150–$220 per hour at typical loaded cost. Multiply by revision cycles before first issue.

§11 — FAQ

Questions engineering leaders ask us on day one.

How do you protect our drawings and IP?
Every engagement starts with a mutual NDA. All project data is stored in encrypted, access-controlled workspaces. Your drawings never leave our environment, and we can work inside your systems (Citrix, VDI, VPN) if required. Individual engineers are bound by confidentiality agreements and work on role-segregated accounts.
Which electrical codes and standards do your engineers work to?
Our teams are trained on NEC 2023 (US), IEC 60364 and IEC 61439 (international), AS/NZS 3000 (Australia/NZ), CSA C22.1 (Canada), and BS 7671 (UK). Every deliverable is checked against the applicable standard for your jurisdiction as part of our multi-stage QA.
What is your typical turnaround on a single-line diagram or panel schedule?
Most single-line diagrams and panel schedules are delivered within 3–5 business days for first issue. Complex MCC and switchgear packages typically run 2–3 weeks. Revisions under our managed-service model are returned within 24 hours.
Do you offer a trial or risk-free first drawing?
Yes. For new clients, we deliver your first drawing as a free sample. If it doesn't meet your internal standards, there's no invoice and no obligation to continue. No credit card required, no retainer — just a scoping call and your source material.
How do you price your engagements?
Three pricing models: fixed-scope project pricing for one-off packages, per-drawing rates (typically 40% below US/AU onshore rates) for ad-hoc capacity, and monthly retainer for managed-service engagements with guaranteed capacity. We'll propose the right model on the initial call.
Where is your team based?
Project management and client-facing engineering leads are based in San Diego, California. Our production drafting and modeling team operates from our Pakistan office, giving clients effectively 24-hour project progression across time zones.
Can you integrate with our existing team and project management tools?
Yes. We regularly work inside client tools including MS Teams, Slack, Jira, Asana, ProjectWise, BIM 360, and client-hosted Citrix / VDI environments. Our onshore PMs run weekly status calls, milestone reviews, and escalation paths alongside your internal PMs.
