REDLINE — The Operating Problem

Normal timesheets do not show drawing progress.

Engineering teams need to know more than hours worked. They need to know which drawing moved forward, who approved it, whether the project is over budget, and what can be shown to a client.

Hours live in one sheet. Drawing progress lives somewhere else.

Connect every entry to a project, drawing, activity, date, description, and approval status.

Progress updates depend on memory and meetings.

Move drawings through configurable stages with completion percentages, comments, deadlines, and history.

Budgets are checked after the overrun already happened.

Compare hours, cost, budgets, approvals, and project activity before cleanup becomes manual.

Leave and availability sit outside project planning.

Track requests, balances, working days, public holidays, approvals, cancellations, and absence history in context.

Managers need proof without making surveillance the product.

Use consent-based screenshot and activity evidence only where verified-hour cases require it.

Morning meetings become status chasing.

Use the planner as the meeting board: yesterday delivered, today assigned, weekly progress visible.

§07 — FAQ

Questions product buyers ask first.

Who is Advanced Mechanix Timesheets built for?
Drafting, engineering, and AEC teams whose work is managed through drawings, document packages, project stages, approvals, and delivery evidence.
What is drawing-level time tracking?
Instead of logging time only to a generic project, staff can attach hours to the actual deliverable: drawing, document, activity, date, description, stage, and approval state.
Does it handle leave and availability?
Yes. Leave requests, balances, working-day calculations, public holidays, approvals, cancellations, and absence history sit inside the same planning context.
Is this surveillance software?
No. Optional consent-based screenshot and activity features can support verified-hour cases, but the core product is planning, time, deliverables, approvals, analytics, and exports.
Where do I start?
Use the company trial flow at staging.advancedmechanix.com, or contact Advanced Mechanix if you want help mapping your existing projects and drawing registers before setup.
