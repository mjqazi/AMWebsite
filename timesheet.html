§01 — The Problem

Your project managers are paid to run projects — not to chase timesheets.

Every Friday, the same ritual: half the crew hasn't submitted hours. The half that has coded overtime to the wrong project. A supervisor approves from memory. Payroll runs late, job-cost reports lie, and a senior engineer just spent three billable hours playing timesheet collector.

Uncaptured and miscoded hours don't just delay payroll — they quietly corrupt every labor-cost report your project decisions rest on. A drawing that skipped checking wouldn't leave your office. Your labor hours deserve the same gate.

Advanced Mechanix runs that gate for you. Our back-office team — the same production organization behind our drafting and BIM services — treats each timesheet as a controlled document: collected, checked line-by-line against your cost codes, approved on the record, and exported payroll-ready on a fixed schedule.

Control principle
A timesheet is a controlled document. It gets a checker, an approver, and a revision log — or it doesn't leave the desk.
  • Missing timesheets chased overnight — closed before your Monday starts
  • Every line validated against project cost codes and pay rules
  • Approval chain run for you, with a timestamped sign-off log
  • Payroll-ready export delivered on schedule, every cycle
  • First payroll cycle free — run in parallel, reconcile it yourself

Three ways to run the desk

Per-Timesheet

Pay per processed timesheet

A per-timesheet, per-cycle rate. Scales with headcount — up in the busy season, down in the quiet one. No monthly minimum.

Best For
  • Crews of 10–75 with seasonal swings

Monthly Desk

Dedicated timesheet desk

A flat monthly fee for a named team that owns your whole cycle — collection through reporting — with a monthly labor-cost pack included. Queries are returned within 24 hours, the same turnaround our managed-service drafting clients work to.

Best For

  • Firms that want the reporting layer too

A fixed written price for defined cleanup work: reconciling historical miscoded hours, rebuilding labor-cost records for a claim or audit, or closing out a project's time records.

§06 — FAQ

Questions operations managers ask us

What exactly do you take over?
Everything between "the field fills in hours" and "payroll runs": collecting submissions, chasing missing timesheets, validating every line against project cost codes and pay rules, running the approval chain, and delivering a clean payroll-ready export on a fixed schedule. Your managers keep approval authority — we do the legwork and keep the log.
Do we need to buy new timesheet software?
No. We work inside whatever you already use — spreadsheets, shared drives, or systems such as Procore, Deltek, QuickBooks Time, or your payroll platform's import format — via client-provided access. If you have no system at all, we set up a simple controlled register inside your own environment.
How do you keep payroll data confidential?
NDA before any data moves. Access is least-privilege and client-controlled — the same Citrix, VDI, or VPN arrangements we use on drafting engagements — with named team members and instantly revocable credentials. Every export passes a four-eyes check, and every touch is logged.
How much does timesheet outsourcing cost?
Two models: per processed timesheet per pay cycle, or a flat monthly fee for a dedicated timesheet desk. Either way it is typically a fraction of the loaded cost of the coordinator or project-manager hours it replaces. We quote a firm price in writing after a scoping call — no surprise invoices.
Is this the same as the timesheet-based billing on your drafting teams?
Related, but distinct. Clients with dedicated Advanced Mechanix drafters already receive itemized, timesheet-based billing as standard — every hour visible. This page is the standalone service where the same back office runs your team's timesheets. Many clients use both.
How fast can we start, and what is free?
Scoping call this week; mapping your cost codes, pay rules, and approval chain takes days, not months — you're live for your first cycle, not your first quarter. We process that first full payroll cycle free, in parallel with your current process, so you can reconcile our output against yours. If it doesn't reconcile, there's no invoice.
Our timesheets are a mess — paper, texts, and spreadsheets. Can you still help?
That's the normal starting point. We consolidate whatever the field actually produces — photos of paper sheets, texts, emails, spreadsheets — into one controlled register. Historical cleanup of miscoded hours can be quoted as a separate fixed-scope backlog project.
