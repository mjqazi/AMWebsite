§01 — Why Perth

WA mining runs on drafting capacity your team doesn't have.

Perth is the operational centre for the largest mining electrical projects in the world. Pilbara iron-ore, Kalgoorlie gold, Goldfields nickel and lithium, Pilbara gas processing — every one of these operations depends on tens of thousands of drawing sheets being produced, revised, and coordinated without stopping.

The constraint isn't design talent. Perth has that. The constraint is production drafting capacity. Senior WA electrical engineers end up doing panel schedules instead of system design, and consultancies bleed margin on junior-heavy drafting desks that can't scale with project peaks.

We built Advanced Mechanix to solve exactly that. Our production team works to AS/NZS 3000 standards, inside Microstation, SkyCAD, or AutoCAD Electrical templates from WA-based clients including tier-1 operators.

§04 — Perth-Specific FAQ

Questions WA engineering managers ask us

Do you work to AS/NZS 3000?
Yes. Every Australian engagement is delivered against AS/NZS 3000:2018 (with current amendments), AS/NZS 3008 (cable selection), and AS 2067 (substations). Our QA process includes jurisdiction-specific code verification as a standard gate.
Can you work to WA mining operator drafting standards?
Yes. We regularly produce documentation for WA mining operators including standards for Rio Tinto, Fortescue, BHP Pilbara, and mid-tier operators. We maintain client-specific cell libraries, title blocks, and conventions per engagement.
Can you handle hazardous area (Ex) documentation?
Yes. AS/NZS 60079-compliant classification drawings, equipment schedules, and installation documentation for zones 0, 1, 2, 20, 21, and 22. We coordinate with your HAZOP and CHAZOP outputs.
What is your turnaround for WA-based clients?
Our Pakistan production team's timezone overlap with Perth (AWST) gives us effectively same-day turnaround on urgent requests. Standard SLDs in 3–5 business days; revision SLA 24 hours on managed engagements.
Can you work through our WA-based consultancy as a subcontractor?
Yes. Most WA engagements run this way — we act as the production drafting team inside your deliverable, under your title block, invisible to your end client. Standard subcontractor NDAs and IP clauses apply.
