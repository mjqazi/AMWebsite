Four-week analytics pilot, production maintenance result.

A mining operation used historian data to identify conveyor motor failure patterns and move from reactive maintenance to targeted intervention.

Project Snapshot

Client: mining operation, anonymized. Scope: historian data review, conveyor motor failure-mode analysis, feature engineering, dashboard prototype, maintenance-priority register, and production rollout support.

The starting point

The maintenance team had years of historian data but little usable warning before repeat motor failures. The obvious answer was not a large AI project. The first need was a practical pilot: prove whether the existing signals could identify failure modes early enough for maintenance to act.

What we tested

  • Cleaned historian tags and aligned motor load, current, vibration, temperature, and stoppage signals.
  • Compared failure windows against normal operating periods and seasonal load changes.
  • Identified a small set of features the maintenance team could understand and trust.
  • Built a dashboard and priority register rather than a black-box prediction score.
  • Defined escalation rules for maintenance planners and reliability engineers.

Result

32%Downtime reduction
19%Maintenance saving
4 wkPilot duration

The pilot justified a production rollout because it gave maintenance planners a specific intervention list, not just an analytics presentation. Six months later, downtime tied to the studied conveyor motor failure modes had dropped by roughly one-third.

Why the pilot worked

The project stayed narrow. We did not try to model every asset in the plant. We selected one failure pattern, proved the signal quality, and made sure the output fit the maintenance team's planning rhythm. That kept the result operational instead of academic.
