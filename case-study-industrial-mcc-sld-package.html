2500 kVA MCC package, zero rework invoices.

How a processing facility received a construction-ready single-line, MCC, and panel-schedule package two weeks early after five coordination conflicts were resolved before mobilization.

Project Snapshot

Client: industrial processing facility in Western Australia, anonymized. Scope: 11 kV / 415 V single-line diagram, 2500 kVA transformer, main switchboard, MCC feeders, panel schedules, cable schedule coordination, and issue-for-construction drawing set.

The problem

The client was preparing to mobilize electrical contractors against a fast-moving plant upgrade. Their internal engineering team had enough source material to brief construction, but not enough clean documentation to issue. The latest single-line was split across markups, vendor drawings, and field notes. Several motor-control feeders were known to have changed, but the changes had not made it back into the drawing register.

The construction window was fixed. The risk was not that drawings would be late in isolation; the risk was that field crews would discover conflicts after cable tray, MCC buckets, and isolation points were already committed.

What we delivered

  • Verified 11 kV / 415 V single-line diagram with utility source, transformer, main switchboard, MCC, and downstream feeders
  • MCC schedule with motor loads, starter references, protection notes, and spare ways
  • Panel schedules and feeder schedule cross-referenced against the SLD
  • Redline register separating client decisions from drafting corrections
  • Issue-for-construction PDFs plus editable source files for the client drawing register

The QA pass that changed the outcome

Our review found five construction-phase conflicts before IFC issue: one transformer data mismatch, two motor-load feeder conflicts, one spare-way assumption that had already been consumed by a vendor skid, and one panel schedule that carried a superseded breaker frame size.

None of those items were dramatic on paper. In the field, each would have triggered an RFI, procurement delay, or rework instruction. The fix was disciplined drawing control: every change had to reconcile across the SLD, MCC schedule, feeder schedule, and title-block revision history before the package moved forward.

Result

5Conflicts caught
2 wkEarly delivery
0Rework invoices

The finished package was accepted into the client's drawing register and issued to the electrical contractor without a rework claim from the documentation scope. The facility also received a cleaner baseline for future arc flash and protection-coordination updates.
