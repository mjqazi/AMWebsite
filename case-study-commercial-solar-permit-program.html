120+ permit packages, without first-submission churn.

A commercial solar EPC needed dependable permit production across California AHJs while its internal engineers stayed focused on design exceptions and utility coordination.

Project Snapshot

Client: commercial solar EPC, anonymized. Scope: rooftop PV layouts, single-line diagrams, equipment schedules, code notes, structural coordination inputs, utility interconnection support, and AHJ-specific permit packages across California.

The constraint

The client had sold more work than its drafting bench could comfortably absorb. The bottleneck was not one complex megaproject; it was the compounding effect of dozens of smaller commercial permits, each with AHJ preferences, utility forms, and revision expectations.

Hiring internally would not solve the immediate queue. Sending work to generic drafting vendors had already produced inconsistent packages and avoidable correction cycles.

The operating model

  • Built a permit checklist keyed by AHJ, utility, project size, equipment family, and mounting type.
  • Standardized title blocks, notes, symbol usage, and drawing order across the client's permit set.
  • Processed packages in weekly batches with exception calls for unusual interconnection or structural conditions.
  • Tracked permit comments and fed those patterns back into the checklist before the next batch.

Result

120+Permits delivered
0%First-submission rejection
5 dayAverage turnaround

The engagement stabilized permit output without diluting engineering oversight. The EPC could keep selling and installing while Advanced Mechanix handled drawing production, package consistency, and AHJ learning loops.

What made it durable

The checklist became more valuable over time. Each reviewer comment, utility preference, and AHJ note was captured as structured operating knowledge. By month six, the program was not just faster; it was less dependent on memory and individual drafter preference.
